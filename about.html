I'm an Instructional and UX Designer, living in Redondo Beach in the Greater Los Angeles area.

We’re all experiencing difficult times right now, recognizing the need for online learning more than ever. As we all know, there has been a major increase toward online interaction due to the global pandemic. This has increased a desire for better quality online learning that will continue long after COVID-19 has gone away. I started the Master of Science in Instructional Design and Technology program in 2020 because I want to use my UX Design background to help improve online educational experiences.

After earning my Bachelor of Science from Cal Poly, San Luis Obispo, I worked in the food industry for eight years. As a Product Development Manager, I oversaw products’ full life cycles - from ideation to launch and post launch. Balancing multiple projects and deadlines reinforced my commitment to effective time management, communication, and organizational skills.

Following my interest in the digital space, I completed the Fullstack Coding Boot Camp at UCLA Extension and a series of courses in UX Design and UX Research. Today, I enjoy making digital experiences better by designing accessible, useful, and intuitive digital products.

My design decisions are backed up with thorough research. I consider users’ backgrounds, including those who may have limited experience with technology. My experience in both design and development helps me appreciate the importance of responsive products and strengthens my interdepartmental communication with developers, designers, and stakeholders.

When I’m not designing and building digital products, I'm enjoying the outdoors, volleyball, pickleball, podcasts, and exploring new food and drink.
